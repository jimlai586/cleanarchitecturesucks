Rx, as in Questionable Experience

Everything from Rx is based on this assumption.

Let’s assume we have a sequence of events.

No, let’s not assume that.

But Disney Star Wars told me to consoom, not ask questions?

Fuck Disney Star Wars.

If I want a string, I return a string. If I want a string many times, I call it many times. The last thing I need is an observer between me and my string, and that observer offers to manage possible sequence of strings for me when I don’t need it.

Even when I do need to handle a sequence of strings, why do I need external dependencies to handle for me? These cases must have association with business logic otherwise why bother. Your millage may vary, but I very rarely need to worry about the particular sequence of some event.

Take networking for example, I post a JSON, and wait for callback. I don’t need to assume some bullshit observer emitting a sequence of bullshit events beforehand, or abstract it away as operator and do composition. No, I want it to callback when I post a JSON. That is it. If I need such functionalities, I would make them. Reading through Rx document just slows me down.

You don’t trade away official SDK for something rarely used.

RxSwift, in its infinite retardation, chose to ignore this. This is a major red flag. Because it then becomes an academic exercise instead of practical application, i.e.; do it for the sake of doing it.

But that’s not even the most questionable part.

To implement observer pattern, at the time of early Swift development, RxSwift has no choice but adopting KVO, which dated back from Objective-C times.

So you went all this way just to bring me a better looking KVO; when I don’t want anything to do with KVO in my Swift codes since Swift is about static dispatch.

You want to inject Objective-C dynamic behavior in a static dispatch language that replaces Objective-C?

Do you even bother with KVO in your Swift code? If not, why do you think it’s cool to have RxSwift in your Swift code?

If it were me, I’ll just wait for Apple to update KVO to be Swift-y. But again, RxSwift’s goal is to port Rx to Swift. Implementation does not matter. Language feature does not matter. They want to build it so they can sell you this Rx, or Questionable experience. They do not give a shit about iOS or its SDK let along developers.

But that’s not even the most questionable part.

Due to technical difficulty, they require developer to manually add reference to a “dispose bag” to avoid memory leak.

You have to manually add code for a reference to be properly released.

In normal production code, in normal code review, this design would be scrapped immediately. Memory leak due to human error is inevitable. No advantage is worth this. Not to mention the boilerplate it produces.

Any developer worth their salt would avoid this like plague.

The retardation of this is off the charts. This alone should tell you RxSwift exists only to sell the Rx brand.

But that’s not even the most questionable part.

The most questionable part, is that there are people who bought all that in, who are so proud of which they begin to replace official SDK with it. In the process introduces more dependency and complexity, and write articles to celebrate their retardation.

It was all personal to me

Look Rx wants to sell their brand. They do everything Rx in every language, and every google search leads you to them. Rx is successful, in terms of marketing or maybe in some platforms better equipped to support it.

But it does not matter what kind of success they have in other platforms, I only care iOS, on which Rx is not good enough.

If you want to try it as an academic exercise or in your own project, it’s fine.

But if you have the audacity, to use it as building blocks in production, and ask people to follow it, then you just declare yourself an amateur and should be treated as such. You should have “maintenance cost” tattooed to your forehead to set an example.

RxSwift developers are often under the illusion of writing clean codes. And because they write the cleanest code there ever is, RxSwift is the standard upon which quality is measured. If you’ve mastered RxSwift, you are the bestest EVAR!!

Bitch, please

You think Rx is your ally. You merely adopted RxSwift.

I was molded by years of hardship learning the in and outs of iOS SDK.

I didn’t know RxSwift until I code reviewed it, by then it was nothing to me but a very expensive way to do callback.

That is what happens when you sit behind a desk. You forget things, like the weight in the hand of a gun that’s loaded and one that’s not.”

When you use Rx, you forget things. Like how to design a library without dependencies; how to make things easier; or how to reduce maintenance cost.

Most importantly of all, you forget (or never learn) how to write high quality code in the context of iOS SDK.

I personally purged RxSwift and MVVM from production by sheer efficiency alone. What’s the point of having RxAlamofire when URLSession gets the job done in fewer codes?

If your view controllers are massive and hard to maintain, it means you suck as a developer. You suck even more if you think RxSwift is an alternative.

Use fucking Combine

First notice that Combine comes with SwiftUI. Unlike RxSwift’s brute force approach to make everything Rx, Combine works with existing or new SDK.

Use situational tool when needed. As all things should be.

Wait for Apple to support it, instead of YOLO it.

Don’t fucking hold onto a dead corpse when Apple actually supports it.

Jesus Christ, I’ve just recently met a guy who claims RxSwift still has advantages over everything else.

Bitch, I could destroy whatever shit you do now with RxSwift using nothing but UIKit. Your shit was obsolete 4 years ago let alone surviving years to come.

Amateurs, so many godamn amateurs. RxSwift is dead, and has never been good. Move on.
